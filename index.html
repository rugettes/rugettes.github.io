3mcanada.ca

A good portion of my current job involves working with JavaScript libraries such as jQuery, RequireJS, and Backbone to solve problems or create new functionality requested by different businesses. One of the first full modules I was tasked with developing regarded a complicated legal matter only applicable to French Canada. Apparently, they have their own version of the trademark symbol (the marques de commerce or MC) that the businesses had been representing in their data with the letters MC in parentheses. However, there seems to be some legal complications with this because it's not technically the correct representation of the trademark, so instead of taking the time to change thousands of product names and hundreds of web pages, it was up to me to write some tricky JavaScript involving regular expressions to analyze any page in the French Canada locale, find all instances of (MC) being rendered, and replace it with its superscript version, sans parentheses.
